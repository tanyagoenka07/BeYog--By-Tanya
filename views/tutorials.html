Stress, Anxiety, and Depression

  • Breathe deeply, allowing your body to relax and your back to stretch gently.

Corpse Pose (Shavasana):

  • Lie flat on your back with your legs extended and arms at your sides, palms facing upward. Close your eyes and let your body completely relax.
  • Focus on your breathing, taking slow, deep breaths, and let go of any tension in your body

Cat-Cow Pose (Marjaryasana-Bitilasana):

  • Start on all fours, with your wrists directly under your shoulders and knees under your hips. Inhale as you arch your back (Cow), lifting your head and tailbone.
  • Exhale as you round your back (Cat), tucking your chin and tailbone toward each other, stretching the spine.

Alternate Nostril Breathing (Nadi Shodhana Pranayama):

  • Sit comfortably with your spine straight. Use your right thumb to close your right nostril, inhale deeply through the left nostril.
  • Close the left nostril with your right ring finger, then exhale through the right nostril. Repeat by inhaling through the right nostril and exhaling through the left.

Back Pain and Spine Issues

Downward Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana):

  • Start on all fours with your hands shoulder-width apart and feet hip-width apart. Lift your hips toward the ceiling, keeping your back straight and legs extended.
  • Press your heels toward the floor, and stretch your arms and legs, creating a V-shape with your body.

Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana):

  • Lie flat on your stomach, with your legs extended and hands placed under your shoulders, elbows bent. Inhale as you press into your hands, lifting your chest off the floor.
  • Keep your elbows close to your body and extend your spine, looking forward or slightly upward.

Bridge Pose (Setu Bandhasana):

  • Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor, hip-width apart. Press your feet into the ground and lift your hips toward the ceiling.
  • Keep your thighs parallel, and clasp your hands under your back, lifting your chest toward your chin.

Seated Forward Bend (Paschimottanasana):

  • Sit with your legs extended straight in front of you, keeping your back straight. Inhale and lengthen your spine.
  • Exhale as you bend forward from your hips, reaching for your feet or shins, keeping your spine long and avoiding rounding your back.

Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

Head-to-Knee Pose (Janu Sirsasana):

  • Sit with your legs extended. Bend your right knee, bringing the sole of your foot to your inner left thigh.
  • Inhale, lengthen your spine, and exhale as you fold forward over your left leg, reaching your foot or shin.

Bound Angle Pose (Baddha Konasana):

  • Sit with your legs extended, then bend your knees and bring the soles of your feet together, letting your knees fall out to the sides.
  • Hold your feet with your hands, sitting up tall with a straight back. Breathe deeply and gently press your knees toward the ground.

Shoulder Stand (Sarvangasana):

  • Lie on your back, then lift your legs overhead, placing your hands on your lower back for support. Keep your legs straight and your body in a straight line.
  • Balance on your shoulders and elbows, with your head off the floor. Hold the position and breathe deeply.

Breathing Exercises (Anulom Vilom):

  • Sit comfortably, close your right nostril with your thumb, and inhale deeply through your left nostril. Close the left nostril with your ring finger.
  • Open your right nostril and exhale slowly. Repeat this process, alternating nostrils.

Digestive Issues

Wind-Relieving Pose (Pavanamuktasana):

  • Lie on your back and pull your knees toward your chest, wrapping your arms around them. Press your thighs into your abdomen.
  • Gently rock back and forth to massage your digestive organs, and hold the position for a few breaths.

Seated Twist (Ardha Matsyendrasana):

  • Sit with your legs extended. Bend your right knee, placing your foot outside your left thigh. Inhale, lengthen your spine, and exhale as you twist your torso to the right.
  • Place your left elbow on the outside of your right knee, and turn your head to look behind you, keeping your back straight.

Bow Pose (Dhanurasana):

  • Lie on your stomach with your knees bent and feet close to your glutes. Reach back and hold your ankles with your hands.
  • Inhale as you lift your chest and thighs off the floor, pulling your legs back, and arch your body into a bow shape.

Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana):

  • Lie flat on your stomach, with your palms under your shoulders. Inhale as you lift your chest and head, straightening your arms and extending your spine.
  • Keep your elbows slightly bent and avoid overextending your back.

Obesity and Weight Management

Sun Salutations (Surya Namaskar):

  • Start in Mountain Pose (Tadasana). Inhale, raise your arms, and exhale as you fold forward, bringing your hands to the floor.
  • Step or jump back to a plank, then lower to the floor. Inhale, lift your chest into Cobra Pose, and exhale to Downward Dog. Repeat the sequence.

Warrior Pose (Virabhadrasana):

  • Start in a standing position with your feet wide apart. Turn your right foot out 90 degrees, and bend your right knee, keeping your left leg straight.
  • Extend your arms parallel to the floor, palms facing down. Keep your torso upright and gaze forward.

Boat Pose (Navasana):

  • Sit with your legs extended. Lean back slightly, lift your legs off the floor, and balance on your sit bones.
  • Hold your shins or extend your arms forward, keeping your body in a V-shape. Engage your core to maintain balance.

Plank Pose (Phalakasana):

  • Start in a push-up position with your wrists directly under your shoulders and your body in a straight line.
  • Engage your core and legs to keep your body aligned. Hold the position, keeping your head, neck, and spine in one line.

Arthritis and Joint Pain

Tree Pose (Vrikshasana):

  • Stand tall and shift your weight onto your left leg. Place the sole of your right foot on your left inner thigh or calf (avoid the knee).
  • Bring your palms together in front of your chest or extend your arms overhead, keeping your balance.

Hero Pose (Virasana):

  • Kneel on the floor with your knees together and feet slightly apart. Sit between your feet, keeping your back straight.
  • Rest your hands on your knees and hold the position while breathing deeply.

Extended Triangle Pose (Utthita Trikonasana):

  • Stand with your legs wide apart. Turn your right foot out 90 degrees, and extend your arms to shoulder height.
  • Hinge at your hips and lower your right hand to your shin or the floor, while your left arm extends toward the ceiling.

Chair Pose (Utkatasana):

  • Stand with your feet together and raise your arms overhead. Bend your knees as if you’re sitting in a chair, keeping your weight in your heels.
  • Hold the position with your chest lifted and thighs parallel to the floor.

Insomnia and Sleep Disorders

Legs-Up-the-Wall Pose (Viparita Karani):

  • Sit sideways next to a wall, then lie on your back and swing your legs up the wall, keeping them straight. Adjust so your hips are close to the wall.
  • Rest your arms at your sides and focus on slow, deep breathing. Hold for several minutes.

Reclining Bound Angle Pose (Supta Baddha Konasana):

  • Lie on your back and bring the soles of your feet together, letting your knees drop open to the sides. Use cushions under your knees for support if needed.
  • Rest your arms by your sides, palms facing upward, and take deep breaths to relax.

Child’s Pose (Balasana):

  • Kneel on the floor and sit back on your heels. Stretch your arms forward, lowering your forehead to the floor.
  • Breathe deeply, allowing your body to relax completely.

Yoga Nidra (Yogic Sleep):

  • Lie on your back in Shavasana. Close your eyes and follow guided instructions (if available) or mentally scan your body for tension, consciously relaxing each part.
  • Focus on your breathing and remain in a meditative state for 10-20 minutes.

Asthma and Respiratory Issues

Fish Pose (Matsyasana):

  • Lie on your back, then lift your chest by propping yourself on your elbows. Allow your head to gently drop back, creating a deep arch in your upper back.
  • Rest the crown of your head lightly on the floor and breathe deeply.

Bridge Pose (Setu Bandhasana):

  • Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet hip-width apart. Press your feet into the ground and lift your hips, forming a bridge shape.
  • Clasp your hands under your back or keep them flat, opening your chest for deeper breathing.

Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana):

  • Lie on your stomach and place your hands under your shoulders. Inhale and lift your chest while keeping your elbows slightly bent.
  • Expand your chest as you breathe deeply, keeping your shoulders away from your ears.

Breathing Practices (Pranayama):

  • For Kapalbhati, sit comfortably and exhale forcefully through your nose while pulling your abdomen inward. Repeat in quick, rhythmic breaths.
  • For Bhastrika, inhale and exhale rapidly and forcefully through both nostrils, expanding and contracting your chest with each breath.

Menstrual Cramps and PCOS

Reclining Bound Angle Pose (Supta Baddha Konasana):

  • Lie on your back, bring the soles of your feet together, and allow your knees to drop open. Support your knees with pillows if needed.
  • Rest your hands on your belly or by your sides, focusing on slow, deep breaths.

Child’s Pose (Balasana):

  • Kneel on the floor and sit back on your heels. Stretch your arms forward, lowering your forehead to the floor.
  • Breathe deeply, allowing your body to relax completely.

Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana):

  • Lie on your stomach and place your hands under your shoulders. Inhale as you lift your upper body, opening your chest and stretching your abdomen.
  • Avoid straining your lower back by keeping the lift gentle.

Butterfly Pose (Baddha Konasana):

  • Sit with your legs bent and the soles of your feet together. Hold your feet with your hands and let your knees drop toward the floor.
  • Sit up straight and gently flap your legs up and down like butterfly wings or hold the position while breathing deeply.
